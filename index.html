About Me

My name is Richard. I am a self-taught software engineer in Seattle. Ever since I played Hangman on an old Mac as a kid, I’ve loved computers. Since then, I’ve used computers in one way or another and have helped many of my co-workers, friends, family, and employers be more productive, accurate, and efficient by showing them ways to automate tasks and make the computer do the work for them.

It wasn’t until a couple of years ago, however, when I found my passion for computer science. One of my co-workers suggested that I might like computer programming since she felt that I ‘have the brain for solving complicated problems’ - she was right. I took a few side courses in web development and immediately knew that this was what I wanted to do with my life. Since then I have made time to continue learning despite working a full-time and a part-time job, making improvements to the house that my wife and I just bought, and raising our three boys (you can see a full list of the courses I have taken here). I have learned a bunch of different languages but my favorites are Java, PHP, and Python.

But it’s not all work. I also go to Sounders soccer matches, participate in Tough Mudder obstacle course races, and spend time with my family. I like doing outdoor stuff like hiking and camping, but I also love watching movies, playing video games, and showing my boys how to solve the Rubik’s cube (among others).

After learning non-stop for the past few years, I am finally ready to turn my dream of working in tech into a reality. If you think that I would be a good addition to your company, or if you just want to start a conversation, contact me!

Weather App

This was my first time working with an API (openweathermap.org). Due to the newest geolocation restrictions on Chrome and the lack of free HTTPS support from the API, I couldn't automatically display the weather for Chrome users. However, I chose to still provide them with the best experience possible by adding a search bar that allowed the user to add the city name or zip code, which wasn't part of the original design.

Full-stack Web Application

This project allowed me to graduate from building static non-persistent websites to full-fledged web applications. For this specific application, I used Node.js for the server-side language, Express for route-handling, and MongoDB for the NoSQL database.

Responsive Website
