About Me

My name is Richard. I am a self-taught software engineer in Seattle. Ever since I played Hangman on an old Mac as a kid, I’ve loved computers. Since then, I’ve used computers in one way or another and have helped many of my co-workers, friends, family, and employers be more productive, accurate, and efficient by showing them ways to automate tasks and make the computer do the work for them.

It wasn’t until a couple of years ago, however, when I found my passion for computer science. One of my co-workers suggested that I might like computer programming since she felt that I ‘have the brain for solving complicated problems’ - she was right. I took a few side courses in web development and immediately knew that this was what I wanted to do with my life. Since then I have made time to continue learning despite working a full-time and a part-time job, making improvements to the house that my wife and I just bought, and raising our two boys (you can see a full list of the courses I have taken here). I have learned a bunch of different languages but my favorites are Java, PHP, and Python.

But it’s not all work. I also go to Sounders soccer matches, participate in Tough Mudder obstacle course races, and spend time with my family. I like doing outdoor stuff like hiking and camping, but I also love watching movies, playing video games, and showing my boys how to solve the Rubik’s cube (among others).

After learning non-stop for the past few years, I am finally ready to turn my dream of working in tech into a reality. If you agree and think that I would be a good fit at your company, or if you just want to say ‘Hi!’, you can contact me here. Thanks for stopping by and I hope to chat with you soon.

Weather App

This was my first time working with an API (openweathermap.org). Due to the newest geolocation restrictions on Chrome and the lack of free HTTPS support from the API, I couldn't automatically display the weather for Chrome users. However, I chose to still provide them with the best experience possible by adding a search bar that allowed the user to add the city name or zip code, which wasn't part of the original design.

Quote Generator

For this project, I used JS to pick a random quote from a list and display it. I also used this same type of function to change the color of the background, font color, and twitter logo each type the quote changed. Had this not been created using codePen, I would have had the quotes listed in a database so that only the code for the displayed quote would be included in the HTML.

Responsive Website

This project allowed me to explore SCSS, media queries, flexbox and other advanced CSS concepts while creating a beautiful desktop and mobile website.
